Blogs are dead. Right? They are. The guardian said it. New Republic said it. Forbes said it. Engadget said it. Business week said it. Gigaom said it. Think Progress said it. Gawker said it. ....I could go on. Apparently blogs really are dead. So why (re)start a blog? Is there any value in such an archaic medium? In my opinion, there is. There's a lot of value in it. let me explain.

Prductivity from arguments

Gettings something out of all those internet arguments

Important Issues

There are important issues that deserve more space then a tweet and more visiblity then a facebook post

Flexibility

I can include images, scripts, videos, style stuff, FREEDOM

Permanence and discoverability

Stuff stays, can be found, can be linked to, can be updated, etc

Constructive for other

Tutorials, howtos, problem descriptions, or just interesting ramblings can be useful for others

Moderated comments

having moderated comemtns (or the ability to turn them down) rocks

Single Collection

Having all "important" stuff collected in one place

Writing

Writing is an incredibly useful skill.

Venting

It's nice to have a space to just vent, without size restricktions, without havign to worry about being a like-whore, without hastags or whatever, just speak your mind about a topic.
